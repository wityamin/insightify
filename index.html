Key components of a well-specified task:

  • Title: A brief, clear label summarizing the task.
  • Description: A detailed explanation of what needs to be done to complete the task, including any technical specifics.
  • Acceptance Criteria (or Definition of Done - DoD): The conditions that must be met for the task to be considered complete and acceptable.
  • Estimation: An effort estimate, usually in story points, ideal days, or another unit of measure.
  • Priority: The relative importance of the task, often categorized as High, Medium, or Low.
  • Dependencies: Any other tasks, stories, or external factors that must be completed or available before this task can be worked on.
  • Assumptions: Any conditions or assumptions that are being made for the task (e.g., existing infrastructure, services, or configurations).
  • Notes/Comments: Any additional relevant information, such as links to documentation, design mockups, or important reminders.
  • Task Owner: The person or team responsible for completing the task.
  • Timebox (Optional): A maximum time allocated for completing the task, if applicable.

Key Characteristics of a task:

  • Clear: The task should be easily understood and actionable by the person who owns it.
  • Small and Specific: A good task is small enough to be completed within a reasonable time frame (e.g., a few hours or a day) and focuses on a specific, actionable step.
  • Testable: The task should have clear acceptance criteria that specify when it is complete and functioning as expected.
  • Actionable: The task should describe a concrete action or outcome, rather than a vague or broad requirement.
  • Estimate-able: The task should be something the team can estimate the effort for, ensuring better sprint planning and tracking.
  • Prioritized: The task should have a priority level to help the team focus on what's most important.
